This week's headline
Toast stopped selling software and started selling identity. Competitor🔴 High

Toast is no longer selling features. It's selling a feeling.

Toast · Built for Busy · April 16, 2026

On April 16, Toast launched its biggest brand move since its IPO: "Built for Busy," a national omnichannel campaign built with Arnold agency. Storefront takeovers across NYC, national OOH and radio, influencer partnerships. The hook: being busy means your restaurant is working, and Toast is what makes that possible. Toast IQ is positioned as the operator's "right hand." Carmine's — 3,000 guests a day — is the marquee partner. Toast claims 164,000+ businesses, $2B cash, and Q1 earnings on May 7.

Emotional attachment is harder to break than rational preference. An operator who feels seen by "Built for Busy" doesn't switch when you show a feature comparison. They switch when you show them something Toast can't do for their specific business, or when your relationship runs deeper than their Toast rep's.

Counter emotionally. Get hospitality-native operator success stories into the field before May 7. Toast's QSR enterprise push (Drive-Thru, 15+ location minimum) creates gaps in bars, fine dining, and nightlife. Know your contrast before the earnings call resets the narrative.

Competitor + Guest Data🔴 High

OpenTable told operators to make it their primary system. Operators said no.

Restaurant Dive · WebProNews · April 21–23, 2026

On April 16, OpenTable extended "system of record" language to all clients — mandating it serve as the single source for reservations and guest data, with annual contracts replacing month-to-month. Altamarea Group VP: "We want to have options and multiple partners." Latino Restaurant Association CEO Lilly Rocha went further, flagging that the mandate "could lock in restaurants to certain contracts and products, like point-of-sales systems." KYU is moving exclusively to DoorDash in Miami. The WA AG antitrust division confirmed it's reviewing the changes.

Reps are actively using the LRA quote in FSR conversations and seeing it land. Operators make the POS lock-in connection themselves — you don't have to make it for them.
Use Lilly Rocha's quote verbatim: "annual contracts and platform mandates limit the services and point-of-sales systems operators can use." Then ask: "What does your setup look like if your reservation platform required a specific POS?" Keep outreach active through May.

Macro + Industry🔴 High

The industry just got a gut punch from geopolitics. Here's what Technomic said at RLC.

NRN · Restaurant Business · April 21, 2026

At the Restaurant Leadership Conference, Technomic moved to a scenario-based forecast model — something it rarely does — because standard prediction doesn't work right now. The Iran war drove gas past $4/gallon. The OECD raised U.S. inflation from 3.2% to 4.2%. Traffic was strong in January and February, then slowed to flat in March. Six forces flagged: gas shock, inflation, GLP-1 drugs (1 in 8 adults, reducing alcohol + food consumption), cannabis replacing alcohol for 57% of consumers monthly, structural labor shrinkage, and a new USDA food pyramid. One bright spot: chains that invested in service quality and operations grew 8x faster in 2025.

# beat-clover
Clover
Medium✓ ValidatedNick Arnoni · Apr 22 | Confirmed: Bryce Adams; contract law
New Owner + New Legal Entity = Clover Contract Is Null
When a restaurant is sold with a new legal entity and business name, Clover's existing contract does not transfer — it's null. Bryce Adams confirmed: "That contract is null unless they did a transfer within the sale."
Consistent with standard merchant services contract law. Agreements are tied to the legal entity, not the physical location.
In your NRO pipeline, ask every new owner if they're inheriting a Clover contract. New legal entity = contract null. Move fast before Clover resigns them.
