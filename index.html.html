This week's headline
Toast stopped selling software and started selling identity. Your prospects are losing sleep over inflation, a gas shock, and a reservation platform that may be locking them into a POS they didn't choose. Competitor🔴 High

Toast is no longer selling features. It's selling a feeling.

On April 16, Toast launched "Built for Busy" — a full national brand repositioning developed with Arnold agency, featuring NYC storefront takeovers, national OOH, and a clear emotional hook: being busy is proof that your restaurant is working. Toast IQ is positioned as the operator's "right hand." Carmine's (3,000 daily guests) is the marquee partner. Toast now claims 164,000+ businesses and $2B in cash on hand.

Emotional attachment is harder to break than rational preference. An operator who feels seen by this messaging doesn't switch when you show them a feature comparison — they switch when you show them something Toast can't do for their specific business.

Counter emotionally. Get hospitality-native operator success stories into the field before May 7. Toast's QSR enterprise push (Drive-Thru, 15+ location minimum) creates a gap in bars, fine dining, breweries, and nightlife. That's SpotOn's ground.

Competitor + Guest Data🔴 High

OpenTable told operators to make it their primary system. Operators said no.

On April 16, OpenTable extended "system of record" language to all clients — mandating it serve as the single source for reservations, tables, and guest data, with annual contracts replacing month-to-month. Altamarea VP: "We want to have options." Latino Restaurant Association CEO Lilly Rocha went further, explicitly flagging the mandate "could lock in restaurants to certain contracts and products, like point-of-sales systems." KYU is now exclusively on DoorDash in Miami. WA AG antitrust division confirmed it's reviewing the terms.

Reps using the LRA quote in FSR conversations are seeing it land immediately. Operators make the POS lock-in connection themselves — you don't have to.
Use the LRA quote verbatim. Then ask: "What does your setup look like if your reservation platform required a specific POS?" Keep outreach active through May — enforcement or non-enforcement is the next trigger.

Medium✓ ValidatedNick Arnoni · Apr 22 | Standard contract law
New Owner + New Business Entity = Clover Contract Is Null
Merchant services agreements are tied to the legal entity, not the physical location. A new LLC is not bound by the prior owner's contract unless it specifically assumed it. Bryce Adams confirmed. Move fast — Clover will try to resign them.
In your NRO pipeline: ask every new restaurant owner if they're inheriting a Clover contract. New legal entity = clean break. Don't let Clover resign them first.
